# Discovery Call Script

## Opening

Thanks for taking the time. I am researching a narrow workflow for post-acute teams: building source-cited Medicare Advantage appeal packets from denial letters and clinical documentation.

I am not selling generic prior authorization software. I am trying to understand whether the appeal packet assembly step is painful enough to deserve a dedicated tool.

## Questions

1. What type of organization are you: SNF, IRF, LTCH, hospital discharge, or other?
2. Roughly how many MA patients do you handle monthly?
3. How many MA admission or continued-stay denials did you get last month?
4. Which denial reason appears most often?
5. What percent of denials do you appeal?
6. Why are some not appealed?
7. Who assembles the packet?
8. How long does it take?
9. What documents are hardest to find?
10. Who has to approve/sign?
11. How do you submit: portal, fax, email, other?
12. How do you track outcomes?
13. What percent are overturned?
14. What is the financial/clinical impact of a lost admission or lost covered days?
15. Would a 10-minute source-cited packet generator change behavior?
16. What would you need to see to pilot?
17. Could you provide de-identified historical cases?

## Show concept

Show:
- denial parser;
- evidence map;
- quote drawer;
- missing checklist;
- packet preview.

Do not over-demo. Listen for pain.

## Closing

Based on what you shared, I would like to test this on 3-5 de-identified historical cases. The output would be an evidence map, packet draft, missing evidence checklist, and time-savings estimate. Would that be useful enough to schedule a follow-up?
