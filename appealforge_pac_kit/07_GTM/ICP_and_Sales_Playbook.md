# ICP and Sales Playbook

## Ideal customer profile

### Best first customers
Regional post-acute operators with:
- 3-50 SNF facilities;
- meaningful Medicare Advantage census;
- centralized or semi-centralized utilization review/revenue cycle;
- recurring admission or continued-stay denials;
- executive frustration with denial delays;
- access to de-identified historical cases.

### Avoid early
- massive enterprise health systems with long procurement;
- payer-owned post-acute providers;
- organizations with no MA volume;
- buyers demanding full EHR/payer integration before pilot;
- customers unwilling to provide historical de-identified packets.

## Outreach positioning

Subject lines:
- "Are MA SNF denials getting appealed at your facilities?"
- "95% SNF denial overturn signal — quick question"
- "Can your team build a denial appeal packet in 10 minutes?"

Opening:
> I'm building a narrow workflow for post-acute teams: turn a Medicare Advantage denial letter and clinical packet into a source-cited appeal packet for staff/physician review. The wedge is SNF admission and continued-stay denials.

Ask:
> Would you be open to testing it on 3-5 de-identified historical denial packets?

## Discovery call script

1. How many MA denials did you see last month?
2. Which denial type hurts most: admission, continued stay, IRF, home health, DME?
3. What percent are appealed?
4. Who builds appeal packets?
5. How long does one packet take?
6. What evidence is hardest to find?
7. What percent get overturned?
8. What is the cost of a lost admission or lost covered days?
9. What systems are involved?
10. Would you pilot if your historical cases show time savings?

## Objection handling

### "We already have an RCM system."
Response:
> Great. AppealForge is not replacing RCM. It fills the clinical evidence assembly gap between denial notice and appeal packet.

### "We use payer portals."
Response:
> V1 does not replace payer portals. It gives your team a better packet to upload/fax.

### "We cannot trust AI."
Response:
> The AI cannot submit or make uncited clinical claims. Every clinical statement is draft, source-cited, and review-gated.

### "We need EHR integration."
Response:
> Long term, yes. The first pilot works on de-identified historical PDFs so we can prove value before integration.

### "Compliance will block this."
Response:
> Prototype uses de-identified data only. Production requires BAAs, audit logs, RBAC, no PHI in logs, and human approval.

## Pilot proposal

### 90-day pilot
- 3-5 facilities.
- De-identified historical cases first.
- Then limited production with BAAs if approved.
- Weekly review.
- Metrics dashboard.

### Pilot fee
$1,500-$3,000 per facility per month.

### Pilot success metrics
- packet assembly time reduced;
- more denials appealed;
- reviewer trust score;
- export quality;
- number of missing evidence gaps resolved;
- outcome tracking completed.
