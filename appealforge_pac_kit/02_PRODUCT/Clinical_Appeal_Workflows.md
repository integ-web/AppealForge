# Clinical Appeal Workflows

## Workflow A — SNF admission denial

### Common denial language
- Does not meet skilled level of care.
- Can be managed at lower level of care.
- Custodial care.
- No daily skilled need.
- Therapy does not require SNF setting.
- Insufficient documentation.

### Evidence categories
- Recent hospitalization and reason for discharge need.
- Skilled nursing need.
- Skilled therapy need.
- Functional baseline and current deficits.
- Medical complexity.
- Medication complexity.
- Wound care or IV needs.
- Fall/safety risk.
- Cognitive/behavioral considerations.
- Discharge barriers.
- Physician statement.

### Packet logic
1. State the requested service and denial reason.
2. Summarize clinical trajectory.
3. Map evidence to skilled criteria.
4. Cite exact notes.
5. Identify why lower level of care is unsafe or inadequate.
6. Request reversal/authorization.
7. Attach ordered documentation.

## Workflow B — SNF continued stay denial

### Common denial language
- Custodial care.
- Plateaued therapy progress.
- No further skilled need.
- Stable condition.
- Services can be provided at home or lower level.

### Evidence categories
- Ongoing skilled nursing interventions.
- Therapy progress and remaining goals.
- Safety risks.
- Unresolved medical issues.
- Changes in condition.
- Discharge plan barriers.
- Physician/therapy support.

### Packet logic
1. Show continued skilled need.
2. Show why remaining services require trained personnel.
3. Demonstrate progress or medical necessity despite slow progress.
4. Refute custodial characterization using cited facts.
5. Request continued authorization period.

## Workflow C — IRF admission denial

### Common denial language
- Could be treated at lower level of care.
- Does not require intensive rehabilitation.
- Cannot tolerate therapy intensity.
- Medical needs do not require IRF physician oversight.

### Evidence categories
- Need for intensive multidisciplinary rehab.
- Therapy tolerance/potential.
- Physician oversight need.
- Nursing/medical complexity.
- Functional deficits.
- Comorbidities.
- Expected improvement.
- Discharge potential.

### Packet logic
1. Identify why IRF level is needed.
2. Show functional deficits and rehab potential.
3. Show medical supervision need.
4. Contrast against lower level alternatives.
5. Request reversal.

## Evidence quality ladder

1. Direct quote from denial letter.
2. Direct quote from discharge summary.
3. Direct quote from therapy eval.
4. Direct quote from nursing/medical note.
5. Structured vitals/labs/medication data.
6. Reviewer-entered clarification.
7. Unsupported statement — not allowed.

## Human review ladder

- Unreviewed AI extraction.
- Staff reviewed.
- Clinical reviewer reviewed.
- Physician/medical director approved.
- Packet approved for export.

## Missing evidence examples

- "No PT/OT evaluation found after hospital discharge."
- "No note found documenting wound measurement or dressing frequency."
- "No physician statement found for expedited appeal."
- "No cited evidence found for medication requiring skilled monitoring."
- "Denial letter says lower level of care is appropriate, but no home safety assessment found."

## Safety rule

When in doubt, ask for evidence. Never fill the gap with plausible medical language.
